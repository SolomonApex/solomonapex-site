The Origin

Enough talking. Enough theory. Build something real.

Years before Solomon Apex existed, Jeff Kline ran a coaching program called Walk On Water. The mission was simple: help people who wanted to build a business stop wanting and start doing.

It worked, in the ways that matter. Several students launched real businesses. Some are still operating today. There were real transformations — people who went from "I've been saying I want to start something" to actually selling something for the first time.

But the model had a problem: it ran on Jeff's time. Every transformation required hours of one-on-one work. The people who could afford it got the help. The people who needed it most often couldn't access it.

Walk On Water closed because it wasn't scalable. The coaching was real. The frameworks were real. But the delivery model couldn't reach the people who needed it most.

Solomon Apex is the answer to that problem. The same frameworks. The same process. Now delivered through a system — with AI doing the work that previously required hours of direct consultation — so it's accessible to anyone willing to put in the time.

Who Built This

Jeff Kline — the builder behind the system.

Jeff spent 15 years inside some of the most demanding brand and operations environments in the country. He ran large-scale event programs for Adobe, Cisco, Microsoft, and Oracle. The through-line in all of it: figuring out the system when nobody else had built one yet. Getting large, complex operations to work when they shouldn't have. Finding the structure that makes things run when running seems impossible.

Jeff isn't a social media personality who turned a side hustle into a course about side hustles. He's someone who's been inside real organizations solving real operational problems for a long time — and who believes that the people who most need help launching something are exactly the people who never get it.

Solomon Apex exists because he decided to do something about that.
