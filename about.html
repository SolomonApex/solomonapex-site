The Origin

Enough talking. Enough theory. Build something real.

Years before Solomon Apex existed, Jeff Kline ran a coaching program called Walk On Water. The mission was simple: help people who wanted to build a business stop wanting and start doing.

It worked, in the ways that matter. Several students launched real businesses. Some are still operating today. There were real transformations — people who went from "I've been saying I want to start something" to actually selling something for the first time.

But the model had a problem: it ran on Jeff's time. Every transformation required hours of one-on-one work. The people who could afford it got the help. The people who needed it most often couldn't access it.

Walk On Water closed because it wasn't scalable. The coaching was real. The frameworks were real. But the delivery model couldn't reach the people who needed it most.

Solomon Apex is the answer to that problem. The same frameworks. The same process. Now packaged into a step-by-step methodology you work through using your own free AI tools - ChatGPT or Claude. Accessible to anyone willing to put in the time.

The frameworks come from real operational work, not from watching YouTube and packaging it up.

Solomon Apex exists because he decided to do something about that.

The system. Not a person. Not a SaaS product. A system.

Solomon Apex is a methodology system - a structured process with frameworks and prompts at each stage, designed to move capable people from idea to offer to launch.

Solomon isn't a chatbot you talk to. It's not software you subscribe to. It's not a service where AI does your work for you. The name "Solomon" isn't accidental. The goal of this system is wisdom applied practically - not inspiration, not motivation, not a collection of frameworks. A clear path from where you are to something real.
